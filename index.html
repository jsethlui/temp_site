Our goal is to enable researchers tackle difficult problems concerning high-speed autonomy through scalable methods. To achieve that, we've made our miniature platforms open-source as to encourage autonomous races to race together and have fun!

SCaR x IEEE

In an effort to realize our goal, we've connected with the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) to allow undergraduate students to race their autonomous vehicles against each other. Moving at 24 mph (350 mph to scale) these Multi-Agent Autonomous Cars for High-Speed, Indoor Environments (MACHInE) dominate the highway scenario without the need for any driver.

Collision Avoidance with
Overtaking Maneuvers

With an end-to-end autonomous driving algorithm, the Overtaking Procedure for Collision Avoidance Systems (OP-CAS) is not only cheap on hardware, but also easy to deploy on embedded systems.
